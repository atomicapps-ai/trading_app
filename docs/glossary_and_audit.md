## 1. Glossary — canonical definitions

### Trading strategy
**Definition.** A set of *predetermined rules* that define when to enter,
when to exit, how much to risk, and (sometimes) what to trade. A strategy
is the part of a trading approach that *finds trades* — entry, exit,
sizing — and is expected to be reevaluated and tuned as conditions
change.

**Source consensus.** Investopedia (via secondary echoes), Wikipedia,
financestrategists.com — all converge: rules for selecting and executing
trades. Components: assets, entry/exit conditions, position sizing, risk
parameters.

**Sources.**
- [Trading strategy — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trading_strategy)
- [What is a Trading Strategy — Bajaj Broking](https://www.bajajbroking.in/blog/what-is-a-trading-strategy-how-to-develop-one)

### Trading strategy vs trading system
A **system** is the broader roadmap: tools + strategy + money-management
rules + psychology + journal. A **strategy** is just the entry/exit/sizing
rule-set inside that system. A system is meant to be relatively stable; a
strategy is meant to evolve.

**Sources.**
- [Strategy vs Trading System — MQL5 blog](https://www.mql5.com/en/blogs/post/769089)
- [Strategy vs Trading System — Binomo](https://blog.binomo.com/strategy-vs-trade-system-whats-the-difference/)

### Stock screener
**Definition.** A tool — *not* a result. A stock screener is a filter UI
linked to a directory of stocks; the user supplies criteria (market cap,
P/E, RSI, …) and the screener returns the subset that matches. The output
of a screener is sometimes called a "screen" or a "screened list".

The screener is the recipe; the matched ticker list is its output.

**Sources.**
- [What is a Stock Screener — Investing.com Academy](https://www.investing.com/academy/stocks/what-is-a-stock-screener/)
- [What Is a Stock Screener — AAII](https://www.aaii.com/journal/article/16503-what-is-a-stock-screener)
- [TradingView Stock Screener walkthrough](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000718885-tradingview-screeners-walkthrough/)

### Trading signal
**Definition.** A *trigger for action* — buy or sell — generated by an
analysis process. The analysis can be a human reading technical
indicators, a math algorithm, a fundamentals scan, or a sentiment model.
A signal is the moment-of-action output, not the rule that produced it.

A signal is generated *by* a strategy (or by an indicator threshold being
crossed); it is not a synonym for either.

**Sources.**
- [Trade Signal — Wallstreetmojo](https://www.wallstreetmojo.com/trade-signal/)
- [What is a trade signal — Ventura Securities](https://www.venturasecurities.com/blog/what-is-a-trade-signal-definition-and-benefits/)

### Trade setup
**Definition.** A *specific configuration* of conditions on a chart that
matches a strategy's entry criteria. The setup is the pre-defined picture
of "what a good trade looks like": pattern + context + levels. When the
setup is present, the strategy says "now is the time to act" — that's the
signal.

Setups are the building blocks of a strategy. One strategy can recognize
several setup variants.

**Sources.**
- [The Difference Between Strategy, Setup, and Pattern — StocksToTrade](https://stockstotrade.com/the-difference-between-strategy-setup-and-pattern/)
- [What is a Trade Setup — JournalPlus](https://journalplus.co/learn/glossary/setup/)

### Trade plan (vs trade setup)
A **trade plan** is the broader, written framework — goals, risk rules,
journaling habits, psychology, money management — within which strategies
and setups operate. A **trade setup** is one ready-to-act instance under
that plan. *In this codebase* "TradePlan" is used to mean a single
ready-to-execute order package (entry/stop/TPs/sizing for one ticker),
which is closer to the industry term **trade ticket / trade order plan**
than the industry "trading plan". Worth flagging for naming review but
the model is too widely referenced to rename right now.

**Sources.**
- [Trading Strategy vs Trading Plan — Rockwell Trading](https://www.rockwelltrading.com/articles/day-trading-blog/trading-strategy-vs-trading-plan/)
- [Should I Have a Trading Plan — AvaTrade](https://www.avatrade.com/education/correct-trading-rules/trading-plan)

### Investment universe (a.k.a. "investable universe")
**Definition.** The *full set* of securities a given investor or system
considers eligible for investment. Can be defined by asset class,
geography, liquidity, compliance, or a manager's mandate. The universe is
the haystack; the screener filters it; the watchlist is what you pull
out to monitor.

A common pattern in industry tools: **Universe → Screen → Watchlist →
Position**. Each step narrows the set.

**Sources.**
- [Universe of Securities — Finance Strategists](https://www.financestrategists.com/wealth-management/investments/universe-of-securities/)
- [Investment Universe — Mid&West glossary](https://midandwest.co.uk/glossary-investment-universe)

### Watchlist
**Definition.** A *user-curated* list of securities being actively
monitored for trading opportunities. Smaller and more dynamic than a
universe, larger and less actionable than a position list. TradingView,
Nasdaq, Investopedia all converge: watchlists are about *attention
allocation*, not eligibility.

**Sources.**
- [Watchlist Definition — Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/glossary/w/watch-list)
- [Mastering TradingView Watchlists](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000745825-mastering-the-tradingview-watchlists/)
- [Watch Lists and Universes — TrendSpider](https://trendspider.com/learning-center/watch-lists-and-universes/) — useful: the only secondary that explicitly contrasts the two terms.

### Chart pattern
**Definition.** A recognizable *shape* in price action — head-and-
shoulders, double bottom, flag, cup-and-handle, etc. — believed to imply
a probabilistic future move. A pattern is a structural observation; it
is not itself a signal until combined with confirmation rules.

**Sources.**
- [Chart pattern — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chart_pattern)
- [Chart Patterns — OANDA](https://www.oanda.com/us-en/trade-tap-blog/trading-knowledge/chart-patterns-in-technical-analysis-a-comprehensive-guide/)

### Technical indicator
**Definition.** A mathematical function over price/volume/open-interest
data — RSI, MACD, ATR, moving averages — plotted alongside the price
chart to support analysis. Indicators are *derived data*, not actions.
Crossing a threshold *on* an indicator can produce a signal; the
indicator itself does not.

**Sources.**
- [Technical Indicator — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_indicator)
- [Technical Indicator — Britannica Money](https://www.britannica.com/money/technical-indicator-types)
- [Pine Script: indicator() vs strategy()](https://www.tradingview.com/pine-script-docs/concepts/strategies/) — TradingView's "study" is just the older name for "indicator".

### Detector (note: this is a programming term)
Not a trading term. In computer-vision and ML it means "a function that
returns presence/absence of a pattern". In trading literature it does not
appear as a standalone concept — patterns are *recognized* or *scanned
for*, not "detected" by a "detector". The closest industry term is
**pattern scanner** or **pattern recognizer** (Finviz, TradingView,
TrendSpider all use "scanner").

In this codebase "detector" has been used as a synonym for "strategy",
which is the principal misuse called out below.

## 2. Term hierarchy (the picture)

The industry mental model — distilled from the seven sources above:

```
                 ┌─ Investment universe ──┐
                 │  (everything eligible) │
                 └────────────┬───────────┘
                              │ filtered by
                              ▼
                 ┌─ Screener / Stock list ┐
                 │  (recipe → matched     │
                 │   subset of universe)  │
                 └────────────┬───────────┘
                              │ user picks attention
                              ▼
                 ┌─ Watchlist ────────────┐
                 │  (curated subset       │
                 │   actively monitored)  │
                 └────────────┬───────────┘
                              │ strategy applies rules
                              ▼
                 ┌─ Strategy ─────────────┐
                 │  rules + recognizes    │
                 │  setups via patterns/  │
                 │  indicators            │
                 └────────────┬───────────┘
                              │ when setup is present
                              ▼
                 ┌─ Signal ───────────────┐
                 │  trigger to act        │
                 └────────────┬───────────┘
                              │ produces
                              ▼
                 ┌─ Trade plan / ticket ──┐
                 │  entry, stop, TPs,     │
                 │  size — order package  │
                 └────────────────────────┘
```

## 3. Codebase misuse audit

### 🔴 "Universe" — wrong meaning
- **Code says:** `universe_filter_presets.yaml`,
  `agents/universe_filter.py`, `templates/universe.html`,
  `routers/universe.py`, `models/universe.py`, sidebar group
  **"Universe"** with children `Stock Screeners` and `Stock Lists`.
- **Code uses it to mean:** a single screener's *output ticker list*
  ("a universe is what you save after running a Finviz scrape").
- **Industry uses it to mean:** the master eligibility set the system
  considers tradable in the first place. A universe is the *input* to a
  screener, not the output.
- **User's stated intent:** make "Base Universe" mean exactly the
  industry definition — "stocks the system has deemed tradable" — and
  reserve a different word for "screener output".
- **Severity:** high. The current "Stock Screeners" page is in fact a
  list of named screeners, each of which has a saved ticker list. That
  saved list is not a universe; it is a *screen result* or a
  *symbol set*.

### 🔴 "Strategy" — smeared across four layers
Today, the concept of "strategy" is split across four places that don't
know about each other:
- **`strategy_configs/*.yaml`** — the cleanest version. Bundles
  detector, thresholds, risk knobs, exit rules, backtest summary.
  (`double_lock.yaml` is a complete first-class strategy in this sense;
  `swing_momentum.yaml` is a generic threshold dump.)
- **`agents/detectors/*.py`** — the user calls these "strategies" in
  conversation; the code calls them "detectors". They are
  pattern-recognizing functions, not strategies. Industry term:
  **pattern scanner**.
- **`workflows/*.yaml`** — DAG runners that bundle screener + analyst +
  portfolio_manager + gates. Closer to the industry term **trading
  system** than to "strategy".
- **`scripts/pine/*.pine`** — Pine Script `strategy()` scripts. Pine's
  notion of "strategy" matches industry: simulates entries/exits with
  backtest results. But they're disconnected from `strategy_configs/`
  even when they're testing the same idea (DL).
- **Severity:** high. This is the single biggest source of confusion
  between you and the user.

### 🔴 "Detector"
- Used 14× in `agents/detectors/`, `models/pattern.py`,
  `agents/analyst.py`, `agents/portfolio_manager.py`.
- Industry term is **pattern scanner** or **pattern recognizer**.
  TradingView calls these "scans"; Finviz calls them "patterns";
  ChartMill calls them "screens".
- User has called out that "detector" is being used as a synonym for
  "strategy", which is the deeper bug — even if we keep "detector" as
  the programming term, it should not be visible in user-facing labels
  or function as a stand-in for the strategy concept.
- **Severity:** medium. Renaming to `agents/scanners/` would be cleaner
  but is mechanical. The bigger fix is making sure detectors are
  *components of* strategies, not interchangeable with them.

### 🟡 "Signal"
- Code defines `Signal` (`models/signal.py`) as the analyst-lens output:
  a per-symbol, per-lens directional read with strength and price
  suggestions. This is *close* to industry usage — it's a "trigger for
  action" — but the model includes lens metadata that conflates "raw
  technical signal" with "consensus-from-multiple-lenses signal".
- Industry would call the lens output a **lens signal** or **factor
  signal**, and reserve **trade signal** for the consensus version that
  triggers a trade plan.
- **Severity:** low. The model is functional and well-named enough; this
  is a nuance, not a bug.

### 🟡 "TradePlan"
- Industry "trading plan" = lifestyle document. Code "TradePlan" = a
  single executable order package (entry, stop, TPs, sizing).
- Industry term that fits the code: **trade ticket** or **order plan**.
- **Severity:** low. The model is referenced in ~30 places; renaming is
  expensive and the current term is clear from context.

### 🟢 "Pattern"
- `models/pattern.py::PatternResult` matches industry usage: the output
  of recognizing a chart shape, with PQS quality score and price levels.
- No misuse.

### 🟢 "Indicator"
- `services/indicator_service.py`, `services/indicator_registry.py`,
  `routers/indicators.py` all match industry: derived math on price/
  volume that produces overlay or oscillator data.
- No misuse.

### 🟢 "Watchlist"
- Used once, correctly: `PatternResult.watchlist_candidate` flags a
  partially-formed pattern that should be monitored but isn't ready to
  trade. Matches industry "list of things to keep an eye on".
- The user's proposed sidebar adds a top-level **Favorites** under
  **Filtered Symbol Sets** that could play this role.
- No misuse, but the term is *underused* — the codebase doesn't expose
  watchlist semantics in the UI yet.

### 🟢 "Screener"
- Sidebar label "Stock Screeners" → /universe is correct: that page
  *does* list screener recipes (stored as `universe_presets` in SQLite).
- The misuse is two layers up: those screeners live under the **Universe**
  group, which is itself the wrong word for what they belong to.

## 4. Recommended renames

For step 2 / step 3 — concrete proposal, ordered by blast radius:

| Old name (user-visible) | New name (proposed) | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Sidebar **Universe** group | **Filtered Symbol Sets** (per user) | Universe should be one specific thing inside, not the parent label |
| Page **Stock Screeners** at `/universe` | **Screeners** (URL stays, label changes) | Already matches the term; just drop the noun-stuffing |
| **"a universe"** (output of one screener) | **a symbol set** or **screen result** | Frees the word "universe" to mean the eligibility set |
| `agents/detectors/*.py` (filename only) | keep — `detector` is fine as a programming term, but **never** surface in UI labels | Detector is the implementation detail; strategy is the user-facing concept |
| `strategy_configs/*.yaml` | **Strategies** (sidebar leaf, replaces stub `/strategies` route) | This is the closest thing the code has to a real strategy object |

For step 3, the model the user is proposing already has a clean home:
keep `strategy_configs/*.yaml` as the strategy definition, and have
each YAML reference (a) which detector(s) to run, (b) which screener
to run them against, (c) which workflow schedules them, (d) which
Pine file is its TradingView twin. That's the "first-class strategy"
the user wants — it's just a join across files that already exist.

## 5. Open questions for the user (before step 2 / 3)

These have to be answered by the user, not guessed:

**Q1 — Favorites semantics.** Is **Favorites** under Filtered Symbol Sets:
- (a) an independent watchlist that can include tickers *outside* Base
  Universe (e.g. a name you want to follow but the system rejected on
  liquidity), or
- (b) a starred subset *strictly within* Base Universe (a "shortlist of
  the universe")?

The two have very different DB shapes — (a) is its own ticker table;
(b) is a `is_favorite` flag on the universe row.

**Q2 — Stock Lists placement.** Currently `/universe/stock-lists`
(S&P 500, NASDAQ-100, FAANG, etc). Three options:
- (a) Keep top-level under **Filtered Symbol Sets** as **Reference
  Lists** — visible to the user, used as input to screeners or as
  ad-hoc browsing.
- (b) Hide them — they become *plumbing* that other features (Base
  Universe construction, screener "starting set" pickers) consume but
  the user never navigates to.
- (c) Both — keep the page, but also let Base Universe pull from them.

**Q3 — Validated / In Progress / Archived triggers.**
- (a) Automatic: a strategy is `validated` iff at least one of its
  validation evidence rows passes the threshold (e.g. ≥72% WR over
  ≥10y backtest); `archived` iff the user marks it; `in_progress`
  is the default.
- (b) Manual: user moves strategies between buckets explicitly, with
  validation evidence as an advisory tag.
- (c) Hybrid: auto-promote to **Validated** on threshold pass, but
  **Archived** is always manual.

The user's wording ("validated = backtest cleared the bar (DL today)")
leans (a) for Validated, but doesn't say what triggers the others.

**Q4 — Strategy template vs instance.**
The user's step-3 proposal says "each strategy is an instance of a
template (e.g. `double_conviction_candle_with_regime_filter`) that
knows how to render itself as both a Pine Script and a Python
detector". That's a strong claim — it implies a code generator. Is
the user actually asking for:
- (a) a literal codegen path (one template → both `.pine` and `.py`
  files emitted), or
- (b) a *paired-files convention* (one YAML strategy points to one
  `.py` detector and one `.pine` file, both hand-written, but the
  YAML is the source of truth for thresholds), or
- (c) just a UI grouping (this Python detector and this Pine file are
  "the same strategy" for display purposes)?

(b) is dramatically less work than (a) and gives most of the benefit;
(c) is a one-evening change.

**Q5 — Cleanup blast radius.**
The 9 swing detectors are filler. User said "remove the rest and keep
DL as the only strategy." Two questions:
- Move to `agents/detectors/_unvalidated/` (preserved, importable for
  later research) or hard-delete (clean slate)?
- `strategy_configs/swing_momentum.yaml` and
  `workflows/{morning,evening,research}_run.yaml` — same question:
  preserve or delete?

**Q6 — `routers/jobs.py` and `routers/strategies.py`.**
Not present in the worktree (`git status` is clean). They may have
been deleted already, or only exist on a different branch. Confirm
before treating them as "throw out and rebuild".
