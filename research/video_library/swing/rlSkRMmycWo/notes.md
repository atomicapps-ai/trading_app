# rlSkRMmycWo — "8 Quant Trading Strategies" (Quantified Strategies)

Source: <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rlSkRMmycWo>

## Rules (as described) — mostly single-instrument index/ETF/seasonal
1. **Russell rebalancing:** buy Russell 2000 the 1st trading day after Jun 23, sell 1st trading day of July (annual seasonal).
2. **Rubber band:** mean reversion on the S&P 500 (rules only partly given).
3. **Money Flow Index:** buy weakness / sell strength on QQQ, 10-day time stop (mean reversion, ETF).
4. **Faber rotation:** monthly, buy SPY/GLD/TLT when 3-mo MA > 10-mo MA (ETF momentum rotation).
5. **Weekly RSI:** buy RSI<15 / sell >20 on XLP (consumer-staples ETF).
6. **Turn of the month:** buy the 5th-to-last trading day, sell the 3rd trading day of the new month (S&P 500 seasonal).
7. Volatility strategy — **premium/withheld**.
8. Bond (TLT) seasonal — **premium/withheld**.

## Verdict: REJECT — index/ETF/seasonal, out of the per-stock rig scope (+ duplicates + paywalled).
Every disclosed strategy is a **single-instrument index/ETF** system (Russell 2000, S&P 500, QQQ, XLP, SPY/GLD/TLT, TLT) or a **calendar seasonal** (Russell rebalancing, turn-of-the-month) — not per-stock defined-risk swing setups with stops, so they can't produce the ≥~100 R-multiple trades / OOS PF the pass bar scores. #4 is the Faber ETF rotation already rejected (videos aenu7yUxTr8, MCOiVEBjFnA); #2/#3/#5 are mean-reversion (the family repeatedly shown marginal on stocks, ~1.0–1.16); #6 turn-of-the-month is a real seasonal effect but a low-vol %-tilt without defined risk; #7 and #8 withhold their rules (paywalled). Nothing here is a distinct, testable, per-stock daily swing edge to adopt.
Status: rejected
