# G29LbG1Xkvw — "How to stop getting faked out" (trendline-break fakeouts)

Source: <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G29LbG1Xkvw>

## Content — avoiding failed breakouts on trendline breaks
- A fake-out = a failed breakout: price looks like it's reversing/breaking a trendline, then
  snaps back and runs the other way (demoed on gold).
- Advice: you can't avoid fakeouts entirely, but you can reduce how often they catch you — via
  waiting for confirmation rather than entering the raw trendline break.

## Verdict: REJECT — discretionary, and largely conceptual
Like its sibling trendline video, everything hinges on a hand-drawn trendline (subjective) plus
a discretionary read of what counts as "confirmation" vs a fakeout. The talk is mostly a
concept explainer ("what is a fake-out") without a numeric entry/stop/target rule set. Nothing
mechanizable beyond generic break-and-confirm, which the live `momentum_breakout` /
`coil_breakout` already implement deterministically. No new testable edge.
Status: rejected
