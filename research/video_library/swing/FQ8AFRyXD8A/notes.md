# FQ8AFRyXD8A — "How to identify the RIGHT liquidity" (stop-hunt concept)

Source: <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FQ8AFRyXD8A>

## Rules (as described)
- **Find significant liquidity pools:** aged, un-tested levels; highly visible levels (prior week/month high, round numbers); **equal highs / equal lows (double tops/bottoms, M/W patterns)** where retail stops cluster.
- **Trade the sweep:** when price sweeps the level and wicks hard back inside ("confirmation candle sniper entry"), enter the reversal; stop beyond the sweep; target opposite side / middle of range.
- **Timing filter (intraday "day-trader clock"):** opening range (9:30–10:00), initial balance (9:30–10:30 ET), London fix (~11:00), avoid midday doldrums (11:30–1:30), watch news "hot zones"; higher-timeframe trend via the 34-EMA.

## Verdict: REJECT — discretionary concept + largely intraday + failed kernel.
This is a conceptual/educational liquidity-and-timing framework, not a codifiable system: "significant," "visible," "confirmation candle," and the whole timing overlay are judgment calls, and the edge leans on an intraday day-trader clock (initial balance, session timing). Its one mechanical kernel — sweep of equal highs/lows then reversal — is the false-breakout reversal already tested for video 4cT8WTyxhYY (long-only OOS PF 1.13, short side negative; below the 1.2 bar). No new testable daily edge.
Status: rejected
