# etCWLTLa6wA — "3 tactics for the moving average" (educational)

Source: <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=etCWLTLa6wA>

## Rules (as described)
- **Tactic 1:** combine a moving average (his default 50-EMA) with market structure — only call a trend once a "1-2-3 move" prints a higher-high/higher-low (or LH/LL) confirming the break out of consolidation.
- **Tactic 2:** use whichever EMA (20/50/100/200) the *specific market* "respects" most — determined by eyeballing historical bounces.
- **Tactic 3:** "sniper setup" — pull back into the EMA where it aligns with a multi-tested support/resistance zone, then enter on a candlestick confirmation (hammer/engulfing/shooting star); ~1:3 R:R.

## Verdict: REJECT — discretionary MA tactics, not a mechanical system.
A collection of heuristics for using moving averages, not one codifiable strategy. Every step is judgment: which EMA a market "respects," where structure/consolidation zones are, whether a "1-2-3 move" or candlestick confirmation is valid. Forex-framed ("currency pair"). No objective entry/stop/exit to backtest. The usable kernel (MA trend filter + pullback) already exists in the suite (fear_dip_reversion) and was tested break-even for video AKkeB8RJ6jM. Nothing new.
Status: rejected
