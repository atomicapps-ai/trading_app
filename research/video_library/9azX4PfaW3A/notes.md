# 9azX4PfaW3A — "Avoid false breakouts / liquidity" (ICT liquidity sweep + FVG)

Source: <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9azX4PfaW3A>

## Content — ICT liquidity / stop-hunt concept
- Equal highs / equal lows are **liquidity** (clustered stop-losses), not support/resistance.
- Price is drawn to that liquidity; a break of the level triggers stops, big players fill
  against them, and price **reverses → false breakout**.
- Confirmation concept: a **fair value gap (FVG)** — an imbalance left by a large candle
  (mark wick-before to wick-after) — as the reversal / entry area.
- Delivered partly as a Discord "trade idea" callout (entry/levels/SL screenshots).

## Verdict: REJECT — discretionary ICT liquidity concept, sweep-reversal already marginal
The entry rests on discretionary reads: which equal highs/lows count as the liquidity pool,
whether a wick constitutes a genuine sweep, and which FVG to trust — none of it numerically
defined, and no fixed stop/target rule. This is the same ICT liquidity/stop-hunt + FVG family
already rejected in this run (e.g. FQ8AFRyXD8A), where the mechanical **sweep-reversal kernel
was tested and came back marginal**. The FVG-continuation variant is separately represented by
the live `fvg_continuation` strategy. Nothing new and mechanizable here beyond concepts already
tested (marginal) or already live.
Status: rejected
