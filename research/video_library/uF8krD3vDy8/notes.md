# uF8krD3vDy8 — "Mean reversion trading" (concept explainer)

Source: <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uF8krD3vDy8>

## Content — a conceptual overview of mean reversion
- Prices return to a "mean"; trade the extremes (or trade toward/away from the mean).
- Explicitly notes the mean is **subjective** ("different for different traders" — a moving
  average, oscillator extremes, or a trendline) and across many timeframes. Discusses how price
  behaves differently in trends vs ranges; "requires a lot of observation and practice."

## Verdict: REJECT — explainer, no fixed mechanical rule
The video itself calls the core choice (how to define the mean) the "most subjective part," and
never commits to a single numeric entry/stop/target. It's education about the *idea* of mean
reversion, not a codeable system. The mechanical versions of this idea are already validated in
this project — `fear_dip_reversion` (live), the passed BB/RSI (pCmJ8wsAS_w), BB-3SD fade
(FqxEKDxemtI), and Connors RSI pullback (W8ENIXvcGlQ). Nothing new and testable here.
