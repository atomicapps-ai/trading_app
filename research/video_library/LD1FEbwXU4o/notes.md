# LD1FEbwXU4o — "Lower-timeframe liquidity" (5-min liquidity hunts)

Source: <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LD1FEbwXU4o>

## Content — ICT liquidity on the 5-minute chart
- "Market makers push price into liquidity to fill large orders." Liquidity = where retail
  stops sit: wicks, swing highs, equal highs, clean support levels.
- Job: find where the obvious trades / stops are sitting on the 5-min and **do the opposite** —
  anticipate the reversal after the stop-hunt. 1-min / 5-min "sniper" entries.

## Verdict: REJECT — discretionary ICT liquidity, no mechanical rules
This is the lower-timeframe version of the liquidity/stop-hunt concept already rejected multiple
times in this run. "Find where the obvious trades are sitting and do the opposite" is a
discretionary read — which highs/lows are the real liquidity, when the hunt is complete, and
where to enter/stop/target are all judgment calls with no numeric definition. The mechanizable
cousin (a sweep-and-reverse) was already tested and came back marginal; nothing new to validate.
Status: rejected
