# _dcYd5T1Lyg — "Trading Weekend Gaps: Rebalancing & Sentiment Trades" (forex/SMC)

Source: <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_dcYd5T1Lyg> · ~18 min.

## Rules (mechanical)
- entry: Two plays off a weekend gap. (1) Gap-fill ("rebalancing") trade: after a gap, sell from a "supply zone" (small-body indecision candle before an impulsive down-move) / buy from a demand zone, targeting the gap fill, gated by a "standard confirmation" = market shifts from HH/HL to LL/LH (a sweep of the prior swing then a close beyond the opposite swing). (2) Sentiment/continuation trade: after the gap fills, trade in the gap's original direction (gap up → buy demand zone toward new highs; gap down → sell supply zone toward new lows), again gated by a standard confirmation.
- exit/stop/target: Stop above the swing high (for sells) / below swing low (for buys). Target = gap fill (trade 1) or prior extreme / new high-low (trade 2). Examples quote ~3.85%, 5.45%, 6.41% moves at 1% risk.
- filters/params: Supply/demand zones, imbalance/"extreme" zone selection, swing-structure breaks. No fixed indicator periods. Sell-limit/buy-limit placement at the zone.

## Verdict: 🟡 SHELVED-INTRADAY — real mechanical structure (gap + confirmation + zone entry), but it is demonstrated on FX/intraday weekend gaps and leans on hand-identified supply/demand zones and swing-structure breaks we can't cleanly mechanize.
Daily analog worth noting: a classic daily gap-fill mean-reversion on US stocks — fade an opening gap of ≥X% back toward the prior close (target = prior close, stop beyond the gap extreme), or a gap-and-go continuation filtered by trend; that analog is mechanizable but the video's zone/confirmation discretion and FX framing aren't directly portable, and it would need its own spec.
