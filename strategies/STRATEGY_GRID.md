### Acceptance criterion (clarified 2026-06-29)
Accept on **out-of-sample expectancy (R/trade) + profit factor**, net of cost, that **beats the random-direction
control** and is **robust** (OOS≈IS, parameter plateau, adequate n) — AND either beats the deployed pair in its
family OR **adds diversification** (monthly-R correlation with the existing book below ~0.4). **Win rate is not a
criterion**: breakeven win = 1/(1+RR), so a 48%-win/1.5R strategy (+0.13R) is worse than a 30%-win/large-RR trend
strategy (+0.91R). High win rate usually just means small capped wins.

## Bottom line
- **Two deployable winners**, both from the Lance/k-X0164r66U video: **Fear-Dip Reversion** (the
  stronger, especially in fear regimes) and **Momentum Breakout**. Live on paper.
- **Three marginal** daily strategies (50-MA reversion, Fib-50 pullback, supply/demand) — real but
  thin; not worth deploying over the two winners (and 50-MA reversion overlaps Fear-Dip).
- **One rejected** (presidential cycle) and **seven shelved** (intraday FX) and **one untestable**.
- Recurring lesson, now across the whole library: **edge = payoff geometry + selection, not the
  raw signal.** Most "strategies" are coin-flips at the trigger; the money is in stop/target
  geometry and skipping junk setups.
